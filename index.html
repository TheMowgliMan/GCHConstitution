Constitution of the Frondosian Empire (Shieveshinik)

Preamble

The ten of us here, in order to express our separation from Shievenisk, now write this Constitution to specify how that will come about. In addition, we now specify here how the system of government used by the new country shall work. We shall ensure the rights of individuals, and recognize the power of the many.

Article I

Section 1.

In order to become separate from the nation of Shievenisk, Shievenisk will relinquish the land that we ask for, no more than one-fourth of her landmass.

Shievenisk will take no part in controlling the new nation.

Section 2.

We shall name this new country, the Frondosian Empire (Shieveshinik), which may be abbreviated Shieve in this and other formal documents.

We shall not change the name of Shieve for 20 years, and at the end of that period the country may be renamed by the appropriate powers.

Section 3.

Shieve and Shievenisk will not go to war for 20 years. At the end of that period, normal war between the two states is permitted.

We reserve the right to have men to guard the border.

Article II

Section 1.

Preamble

The basics of government shall here be laid out, in this article.

The Government is run by a monarch, whose powers are limited. He handles the top-level dealings with other countries and executes the office of government.

Part I

The master of the government is the executive King of Shieve. He runs the government, and has the powers listed below.

Part II

The King has the following powers:

The King may declare war with other nations.

The King may put forth a bill to amend this constitution.

The King may add Federal laws that do not infringe on the rights of the Provinces or the people and that do not conflict with this constitution or the Governmental Bureaucracy.

He may reverse a law put forward by a previous king 20 years after it was put forth.

The King may reverse a law put forward by the Governmental Bureaucracy 30 years after it was put forth.

The King must handle relations with other countries.

Part III

The King is elected by the people of Shieve after every previous king dies. He is elected from the eldest adults from the youngest generation from the families that make up the Governmental Bureaucracy.

Until the election ends, the oldest member of the Governmental Bureaucracy takes the place of the King.

Section 2.

Preamble

The rest of the Government is handled by the Governmental Bureaucracy. The Governmental Bureaucracy is a panel of nine aristocrats who manage the rest of the country not under control by the King.

Part I

When the elections come up for the Governmental Bureaucracy, any person may enter. These people get one speech before the elections start, and one after they are elected, if they are. They are not allowed to have other propaganda.

After all the people who are to be elected have neutered, voting may begin. Every person may vote, regardless of land ownership, gender, race, or wealth. People may vote for up to three candidates. They may hand in an empty vote.

Part II

The Governmental Bureaucracy has a single six-year term for each member. Thus, they will not worry about being reelected and will focus entirely on running the country. They may run again ten years after their term ends. One person may not serve more than four terms in a lifetime.

Part III

The Governmental Bureaucracy has a certain set of powers to be outlined below. Unless specified, all actions require a majority vote on the same item to be passed.

Part IV

The Governmental Bureaucracy has the powers outlined below:

The Bureaucracy can pass Federal Laws that do not infringe on the rights of the Provinces and the People and that do not conflict with this constitution.

The Bureaucracy may reverse a law made by the King at any point, with a two-thirds vote.

The Bureaucracy may reverse a law made by a king 10 years after it was put forth with a majority vote.

The Bureaucracy may approve a bill to amend this Constitution put forth by the King with a two-thirds vote.

The Bureaucracy may suggest courses of action to the King, without a vote.

The Bureaucracy interprets this constitution.

Article III

Preamble

Article III of this constitution exists to ensure the rights of individuals, of companies, and of the government. The rights of the government override the rights of the individual, and the rights of the individual override those of the company, if they come into conflict.

Section 1- On the rights of individuals

Part I

Individuals will be free from unreasonable search and arrest.

Part II

Individuals may run businesses and own companies. If they are larger than 100 employees, they must be registered with the government.

Part III

Individuals may sue the government for any reason.

Part IV

Individuals have the right to a fair and speedy trial.

Section 2- On the rights of companies

Part I

Companies do not need to be registered with the government if they are 100 employees or smaller.

Part II

Companies may sell goods using any currency they wish.

Part III

Companies can sue the government for any reason.

Part IV

Companies have the right to a fair and speedy trial.

Section 3- On the rights of the federal government

Part I

The federal government may limit and regulate commerce. They cannot completely stop international commerce.

In addition, the federal government may limit what products companies can sell.

Part II

The government can control the economy in any fashion.

Part III

The government can not sue individuals or companies. However, it can start a trial if laws are broken.

Part IV

The government cannot choose which cases may go to court and which to not see.

If there is a national court, the court may decline to see cases. However, a smaller court must see the issue.
